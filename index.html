ABOUT US

The widespread use of plastics in today's world presents a pressing sustainability challenge: What becomes of these non-biodegradable materials when they are ultimately buried deep in our oceans?
We at Ristiko, aim to provide a new type of material to industries dependent on metal resources: Metal Plastic Composites (MPCs). MPC refers to materials that combine metal and plastic components to leverage the strengths of both. Typically, this involves layers of metal and plastic bonded together, enhancing properties like strength, weight, and corrosion resistance. Not only would MPCs reinforce the industries dependant on metals, it would help in reuse of plastics- emphasizing on plastic present in oceans, as at our company, all plastics used in our MPC production is extracted from the oceans!

Under extreme temperatures and pressures, metals found in the seabed combine with these plastic components to create metal-plastic composites (MPCs). Considering the remarkable potential of MPCs and their profitable applications across industries including automotive ( in making vehicular parts for noise cancellation), aerospace, weaponries and construction, we provide the extraction of plastics at a reasonable price. Our Collaboration with b2b Companies considering the increasing demand for lightweight, durable materials fuels market growth.

For instance, in the aerospace industry, rockets must endure the intense heat generated during atmospheric re-entry from outer space. They require materials capable of withstanding such extreme conditions to protect both astronauts and the spacecraft's delicate structure. The plastic layers within MPCs not only safeguard the metal from harsh elements but also provide a lighter alternative to pure metal, making them ideal for use in rockets, where weight balance is crucial.

KNOW THE PROCESS

Material Selection:

Choosing appropriate types of metals (like aluminum or steel) and polymers (such as polyethylene or polypropylene).

Surface Preparation:

The metal surfaces are cleaned and often treated to enhance adhesion, such as through sandblasting or chemical etching.

Mixing:

The metal and plastic components are mixed, often using methods like extrusion or injection molding. In some cases, metal powders are combined with a polymer matrix.

Molding or Shaping:

The mixture is then formed into desired shapes using techniques like compression molding, casting, or 3D printing.

Curing or Solidification:

The composite is cured or allowed to cool, depending on the type of polymer used.

Finishing:

Finally, the composite may undergo machining, sanding, or other finishing processes to achieve the required specifications.
